How it works

Three steps to total
financial clarity

No accounts, no syncing, no sharing your bank login. Just upload, analyse, and understand.

01
🏦
Export your bank statement
Log into your bank's online portal and download your transactions as a CSV file. Takes less than 60 seconds. (Currently supports TD Bank customers — more banks coming soon)
02
⚡
Upload & auto-categorise
Drop your CSV into Spendwise. Our smart engine instantly reads every transaction and sorts them into categories — food, gas, bills, entertainment and more.
03
📊
Understand your spending
Get a beautiful, interactive breakdown of exactly where your money went. Click any category to drill down into individual transactions. Edit anything that's off.
Features

Everything you need,
nothing you don't

Built for real people who just want to understand their money — not another subscription you forget to cancel.

🤖
Smart auto-categorisation
Transactions are automatically sorted into meaningful categories using intelligent keyword matching — no manual tagging needed.
✏️
One-click editing
Something miscategorised? Hit edit on any transaction row and reassign it in seconds. Your corrections apply instantly.
🔍
Category drill-down
Click any spending category to filter and see only those transactions. Understand not just the total, but every single purchase behind it.
🔒
100% private
Your financial data never leaves your device. Everything runs entirely in your browser — no servers, no accounts, no data collection.
📱
Works on any device
Whether you're on a laptop, tablet, or phone, Spendwise looks great and works perfectly on every screen size.
🏦
Multi-bank support coming
Currently works with TD Bank CSV exports. Support for RBC, Scotiabank, CIBC, BMO and more is on the roadmap. (Currently TD only)
